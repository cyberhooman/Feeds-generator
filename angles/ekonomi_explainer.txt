## CONTENT ANGLE: Ekonomi Explainer (Economics Breakdown)

**Framework:** Hook with controversy/data ‚Üí Explain the "why" ‚Üí Show impact ‚Üí Reveal the system ‚Üí Actionable insight

**Why It Works:** People are curious about economics but intimidated by jargon. Breaking it down with memes and casual language makes complex topics shareable. The "hidden truth" angle creates engagement.

**Reference Style:** @theeconomicinfluence.id

---

## STRUCTURE:

### Slide 1: THE HOOK
Start with something that makes people stop scrolling.

Options:
1. **Shocking Data Point**
   - A statistic that seems unbelievable
   - Contrast that highlights inequality
   - Number that hits home personally

2. **Relatable Pain Point**
   - Something everyone experiences (harga naik, gaji stuck)
   - Common frustration with the system
   - Personal finance struggle

3. **Controversial Take**
   - Challenge common belief
   - Question popular policy
   - "Unpopular opinion" format

4. **Meme-Style Setup**
   - POV format
   - "Nobody: / [Subject]:" format
   - "[X] be like:" format

**Hook Formulas:**
- "[Shocking stat]. Let that sink in."
- "POV: Lu baru tau kenapa [common problem]"
- "Unpopular opinion: [controversial economic take]"
- "[Authority] bilang [claim]. Reality: [contrast]"
- "Fun fact yang gak fun:"

### Slide 2: THE CONTEXT
Set up what people THINK is true vs reality.

Include:
- The common belief/narrative
- Why people believe this
- What they're not seeing
- "Here's what they don't tell you..."

### Slide 3: THE EXPLANATION
Break down the actual mechanics.

Make it digestible:
- Simple cause ‚Üí effect
- Analogy to everyday life
- Step by step breakdown
- Visual if possible (numbers, comparisons)

### Slide 4: THE DATA/PROOF
Show receipts.

Present:
- Specific numbers
- Before/after comparisons
- Source credibility (tanpa jadi boring)
- Clear visualization

### Slide 5: THE IMPACT
Make it personal - how does this affect the reader?

Connect to:
- Their daily life
- Their wallet
- Their future
- Their generation

### Slide 6: THE SYSTEM REVEAL
Pull back - show the bigger picture.

Reveal:
- Who benefits from current system
- Why it's designed this way
- Historical context if relevant
- Structural issues

### Slide 7: THE TAKEAWAY
What should they know/do?

Provide:
- Key insight to remember
- Practical action (if applicable)
- Mindset shift
- Conversation starter

### Slide 8 (Optional): THE CTA
Drive engagement.

Options:
- Poll question
- Debate prompt
- Share request
- Follow-up topic vote

## WRITING EXAMPLES:

### Example 1: Inflation Explainer

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
```
Gaji lu naik 5% tahun ini.

Inflasi naik 6%.

Congrats, lu technically makin miskin. üéâ
```

**Slide 2 (Context):**
```
"Tapi kan gaji naik!"

Iya. Naik.

Tapi kalau harga barang naik LEBIH TINGGI dari kenaikan gaji...

Lu actually bisa beli LEBIH SEDIKIT dari tahun kemarin.

Ini yang namanya "real wage decline".
```

**Slide 3 (Explanation):**
```
Simple math:

Tahun lalu: Gaji 5 juta, beras 10rb/kg
= Bisa beli 500 kg beras

Tahun ini: Gaji 5.25 juta (+5%), beras 11rb/kg (+10%)
= Bisa beli 477 kg beras

Gaji NAIK tapi purchasing power TURUN.

Ini realita inflasi.
```

**Slide 4 (Data):**
```
Data Bank Indonesia:

Inflasi kumulatif 5 tahun terakhir: ~25%
Kenaikan UMR rata-rata: ~15%

Gap: 10%

Artinya: Daya beli lu turun 10% dalam 5 tahun.

Padahal lu kerja sama kerasnya (atau lebih keras).
```

**Slide 5 (Impact):**
```
Efeknya ke lu:

- Nabung makin susah
- Target financial mundur terus
- Lifestyle yang sama = butuh duit lebih
- Rumah? Makin jauh dari jangkauan

Dan ini bukan salah lu "kurang kerja keras".

Ini structural.
```

**Slide 6 (System Reveal):**
```
The thing is...

Inflasi gak affect semua orang sama.

Yang punya aset (rumah, saham, bisnis) = kekayaan naik sama inflasi.

Yang cuma punya gaji = purchasing power turun.

The rich get richer.
Literally by design.
```

**Slide 7 (Takeaway):**
```
Key insight:

Gaji naik ‚â† jadi lebih kaya.

Yang penting: apakah kenaikan gaji lu BEAT inflasi?

Kalau gak? Lu technically mengalami penurunan pendapatan.

Now you know.
```

**Slide 8 (CTA):**
```
Pertanyaan:

Kenaikan gaji lu tahun ini berapa %?

Share di comment.

Kita liat bareng apakah beat inflasi atau boncos.
```

### Example 2: Housing Crisis Explainer

**Slide 1 (Hook):**
```
Harga rumah: naik 15% per tahun.
Gaji: naik 5% per tahun.

Kapan lu bisa beli rumah?

Spoiler: makin lama makin jauh. üìâ
```

**Slide 2 (Context):**
```
Generasi orang tua kita:

- Kerja 5-10 tahun
- Bisa beli rumah cash/KPR ringan
- "Asal kerja keras pasti bisa"

Generasi kita:

- Kerja 10+ tahun
- Masih ngekos/ngontrak
- Rumah makin gak affordable

Apa yang berubah?
```

**Slide 3 (Explanation):**
```
Simple breakdown:

1990: Harga rumah = 20x gaji tahunan
2024: Harga rumah = 50x gaji tahunan

Artinya:

Dulu: Nabung 5 tahun = DP rumah
Sekarang: Nabung 10+ tahun = DP rumah (mungkin)

Dan selama lu nabung? Harga rumah tetap naik.

Lu lari, target makin jauh.
```

**Slide 4 (Data):**
```
Angka dari Bank Indonesia + BPS:

Rata-rata harga rumah Jakarta: 1.5-2 miliar
Rata-rata gaji fresh grad: 5-7 juta/bulan
Kemampuan nabung (optimis): 2 juta/bulan

DP 20%: 300-400 juta
Waktu untuk DP: 12-16 TAHUN

Belum cicilan 20 tahun lagi.

*laughs in broke*
```

**Slide 5 (Impact):**
```
Real impact ke generasi kita:

- Delayed marriage (gak afford nikah + rumah)
- Delayed having kids
- Mental health issues (financial stress)
- "Sandwich generation" pressure
- Forever renting?

Ini bukan soal "kurang hemat".

Ini soal sistem.
```

**Slide 6 (System Reveal):**
```
Why harga rumah naik terus?

1. Rumah jadi investment vehicle (bukan tempat tinggal)
2. Tanah terbatas, demand naik
3. Developer lebih untung jual mahal
4. Investor/spekulan beli bulk
5. Regulasi yang favor investor, bukan first-time buyer

Rumah bukan lagi basic need.
Jadi komoditas.
```

**Slide 7 (Takeaway):**
```
The uncomfortable truth:

Buat sebagian besar generasi kita,
beli rumah di kota besar mungkin
bukan opsi yang realistis.

Dan itu BUKAN salah lu.

It's a systemic failure.

Tapi at least sekarang lu tau kenapa.
```

**Slide 8 (CTA):**
```
Honest question:

Lu masih target beli rumah?
Atau udah accept alternative? (ngontrak selamanya, tinggal sama ortu, pindah ke kota kecil)

No judgment. Share perspektif lu.
```

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:

‚ùå **Too Academic**
"Berdasarkan teori Keynesian..."
‚Üí Keep it casual and relatable

‚ùå **No Data**
Pure opinion without backing
‚Üí Always include specific numbers

‚ùå **Victim Blaming**
"Ya salah lu gak nabung"
‚Üí Acknowledge structural issues

‚ùå **Too Depressing Without Hope**
Pure doom tanpa insight/action
‚Üí Empower, don't just depress

‚ùå **Oversimplifying**
Complex issues made too black/white
‚Üí Acknowledge nuance where needed

‚ùå **Boring Delivery**
Correct info tapi presented dryly
‚Üí Use memes, humor, relatability

## VISUAL GUIDELINES:

### Slide Design:
- Dark background (hitam, dark blue, dark gray)
- Bold white text
- Accent colors untuk emphasis (kuning, hijau neon, merah)
- Clean, minimal layout
- Max 2-3 text blocks per slide

### Data Visualization:
- Simple bar comparisons
- Before/after side by side
- Big numbers highlighted
- Icons untuk quick recognition

### Typography:
- Bold sans-serif headlines
- Clean body text
- CAPS sparingly untuk emphasis
- Emoji as accent, bukan overload

---

## FINAL TIPS:

**Make Complex Simple:**
If your reader needs a degree to understand, rewrite it.

**Make Dry Interesting:**
Economics is boring. Your delivery shouldn't be.

**Make Anger Productive:**
Don't just make people mad. Give them understanding.

**Make Statistics Stick:**
"10 orang = 50 juta orang" is more memorable than "top 0.001% owns X%"

**Make It Shareable:**
Would someone screenshot this for their story? That's the test.
