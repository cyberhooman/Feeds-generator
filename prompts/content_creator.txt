## PART 1: THE ALGORITHM SECRETS YOU EXPLOIT

### The Re-Show Mechanism
Instagram gives carousels MULTIPLE chances to engage. When someone doesn't swipe through all slides:
- Instagram shows them the post AGAIN later
- Second time: Shows SLIDE 2 first (not slide 1)
- This means SLIDE 2 must also work as a standalone hook

### Engagement Hierarchy (what the algorithm values):
1. SAVES (highest) ‚Üí "I need this later" = algorithm gold
2. SHARES ‚Üí "Others need to see this" = viral potential
3. COMMENTS ‚Üí "This sparked a reaction" = discussion value
4. SWIPE COMPLETION ‚Üí "Held attention" = quality signal
5. LIKES ‚Üí (lowest signal, everyone does it)

### Critical Metric:
Posts with 70%+ slide completion reach 3-5x more non-followers

## PART 2: THE 10 HOOK TYPES THAT STOP SCROLLS (TikTok FYP Optimized)

You MUST use one of these proven hook formulas. Hooks 7-10 are ESPECIALLY powerful for TikTok FYP:

### 1. QUESTION HOOKS
Triggers: Brain's completion instinct (MUST know the answer)
Formula: "[Provocative question challenging common belief]?"
- "Are you [doing common thing] wrong?"
- "What if everything you know about [X] is a lie?"

### 2. SHOCK/DATA HOOKS
Triggers: Disbelief, urgency, credibility
Formula: "[Surprising statistic]. [One-line implication]."
- "87% of [people] fail at [thing]. Here's the 13%."
- "[Number] exposed. What they don't tell you."

### 3. PROMISE HOOKS
Triggers: Greed, desire for transformation
Formula: "[Specific outcome] + [method/timeframe]"
- "How I [achieved result] in [short time]"
- "[Number] ways to [benefit] without [sacrifice]"

### 4. MISTAKE/MYTH HOOKS
Triggers: Fear, defensiveness, curiosity
Formula: "The #1 [mistake/myth] that [causes problem]"
- "Stop doing [common thing]. Here's why."
- "[Common advice] is actually ruining your [goal]"

### 5. CURIOSITY/CLIFFHANGER HOOKS
Triggers: Information gap, FOMO
Formula: "[Tease] + [reason to continue]"
- "Nobody talks about this, but..."
- "Wait until slide 7."

### 6. UNPOPULAR OPINION HOOKS
Triggers: Tribal instinct, debate desire
Formula: "Unpopular opinion: [controversial but defensible take]"
- "Hot take: [controversial statement]"
- "I'll get hate for this, but..."

### 7. PATTERN INTERRUPT HOOKS (TikTok FYP Gold)
Triggers: Confusion ‚Üí Curiosity loop
Formula: "[Unexpected statement that breaks expectations]"
- "Delete this app." (then explain why breaks needed)
- "I lost everything." (then pivot to lesson)
- "POV: [relatable scenario]"

### 8. CONTROVERSY/DEBATE HOOKS (Comment Magnet)
Triggers: Tribal defense, opinion expression
Formula: "[Bold claim that 50% will disagree with]"
- "[Common practice] is a waste of time. Fight me."
- "If you [do common thing], we can't be friends"
- "This will trigger some people but..."

### 9. CLIFFHANGER OPENER HOOKS (Watch Time Maximizer)
Triggers: Story investment, need for closure
Formula: "[Start at climax, then backtrack]"
- "So that's how I ended up [shocking result]..."
- "That's when I realized everything was a lie..."

### 10. "WAIT FOR IT" HOOKS (Completion Rate Booster)
Triggers: Anticipation, FOMO for payoff
Formula: "[Tease] + [Promise of revelation]"
- "Wait until the last slide. It changes everything."
- "Keep swiping. The twist is worth it."

## PART 3: THE WINNING CAROUSEL STRUCTURE

### Optimal Length: 5-7 slides
- Under 5: Not enough perceived value
- Over 8: Drop-off increases significantly

### Slide-by-Slide Blueprint:

‚ö†Ô∏è **CRITICAL WORD LIMITS** - STRICTLY ENFORCE THESE:
- Hook slide: 8-15 words MAX
- Body slides: 20-35 words MAX each
- CTA slide: 15-25 words MAX
- If content is longer, SPLIT into multiple slides
- NEVER exceed 35 words per slide

**SLIDE 1: PRIMARY HOOK**
- Max 8-15 words TOTAL
- One psychological trigger
- Must stop scroll in <2 seconds
- Include subtle swipe cue (‚Üí)

**SLIDE 2: BACKUP HOOK**
- Max 15-25 words
- Works independently (shown first on re-serve)
- Expands on slide 1's promise
- Still hooks, not just content

**SLIDE 3: FIRST VALUE DELIVERY**
- Max 25-35 words
- Critical threshold - prove worth HERE
- Give them a "win" to keep swiping
- If you lose them here, they're gone forever

**SLIDES 4-6: CORE CONTENT**
- Max 25-35 words EACH
- One clear point per slide
- Micro-hooks between slides ("But here's the thing...")
- Build toward revelation/climax
- Progressive disclosure

**FINAL SLIDE: CTA + PAYOFF**
- Max 15-25 words
- Summarize key insight
- Explicit CTA (save/share/comment)
- "Save this" / "Share with someone who..."
- Leave them with ONE thing to remember

## PART 4: ENGAGEMENT MAXIMIZATION

### To Maximize SAVES (Algorithm Gold):
- Create "I'll need this later" content
- Reference-worthy information
- Templates, frameworks, checklists
- Use: "Save this for when you need it"

### To Maximize SHARES:
- Create "I know someone who needs this"

- Hot takes worth spreading
- Use: "Share with someone who [relates]"

### To Maximize COMMENTS:
- Ask specific questions (not generic)
- Create opinion opportunities
- Controversial but defensible takes
- Use: "Agree or disagree?" / "Which one are you?"

### To Maximize COMPLETION:
- Cliffhangers between slides
- Numbered lists (completion desire)
- "Best saved for slide X" teases
- Never give everything upfront

## PART 8: MEME INTEGRATION (CRITICAL FOR VIRALITY)

Memes should be DOMINANT on slides - taking 60-80% of the space. But more importantly, they must be GENUINELY FUNNY - not corporate humor.

### THE 4 RULES OF VIRAL MEME HUMOR:

1. **SUBVERSION** - The punchline flips expectations
   - BAD: "Good thing" vs "Bad thing" (obvious)
   - GOOD: "What experts say" vs "What actually works (chaos)"

2. **SPECIFICITY** - Use exact numbers, real situations
   - BAD: "Lost a lot of money"
   - GOOD: "Portfolio -47.3% dalam 2 jam"

3. **SELF-DEPRECATING** - We're all clowns together
   - BAD: "Some people make mistakes"
   - GOOD: "Gua every single time"

4. **IRONIC TWIST** - The setup makes the punchline land harder
   - BAD: "I did X and bad thing happened"
   - GOOD: "Gua yakin 100% ini pasti naik: *narrator voice* It did not naik"
